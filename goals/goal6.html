Goal 6: Clean Water and Sanitation

Goal 6 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aims to ensure the availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. Access to clean water and sanitation is essential for health, economic growth, and environmental sustainability.

Why Is This Goal Important?

Water is a basic human necessity, yet billions of people still lack access to safe drinking water and proper sanitation. Contaminated water spreads diseases, causing thousands of deaths daily, especially among children. Poor sanitation contributes to environmental degradation and economic losses. Ensuring clean water and sanitation helps prevent disease, promotes dignity, and fosters economic development.

Key Targets:

  • Provide universal access to safe drinking water: Ensure that everyone has access to affordable and safe drinking water.
  • Achieve adequate sanitation and hygiene: Ensure that all individuals have access to proper sanitation, including toilets and handwashing facilities.
  • Improve water quality: Reduce pollution, eliminate dumping, and minimize the release of hazardous chemicals into water sources.
  • Increase water-use efficiency: Promote sustainable water management to prevent scarcity and ensure availability for future generations.
  • Protect and restore water ecosystems: Safeguard lakes, rivers, wetlands, and groundwater sources to maintain a healthy environment.
  • Expand international water cooperation: Strengthen partnerships between countries to improve water management and access.
